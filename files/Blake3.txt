DIDDIE WA DIDDIE
It is said by some Blake-heads that if you can play Diddie Wa
Diddie, you can play any Blind Blake song. It is said by other
more cynical fans that if you can play Diddie Wa Diddie, you've 
already played most of Blind Blake's songs. Indeed, Diddie Wa 
Diddie is an excellent place to start a Blind Blake lesson 
because it involves some great signature licks and eccentric
syncopation that help to outline the true genius of the "pluck-brush"
piano style guitar playing that only Blind Blake ever truly mastered.
It is basically a I-IV-V progression in C with some passing tones
and melodic versing.

4. POLICEDOG BLUES
This is Blake's only song in open E tuning (that I know of)
Ry Cooder does a faithful rendition on his eponymous first album, and 
Jorma Kaukonen does good versions on "Splashdown" and "Quah". Cephas
and Wiggins do an interesting interpretation on "Guitar Man" and 
Stephan Grossman has a few versions out. 

Open E tuning: EBEG#BE (open D also works)

These are the main chords:

11. GEORGIA BOUND-Key of C
This song has the same melody as Robert Johnson's "From
Four Until Late", but with twice the flavor. It is so chock 
full of juicy licks that it defies the repetitive blues format.
While it's similar to "Diddie Wa Diddie" in it's texture, it is
much more melodically diverse and polyrhythmic.
