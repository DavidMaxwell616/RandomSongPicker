This is Bach's Gavotte En Rondeau from BMV 1006a.  BMV 1006a is a lute
suite adaption of Bach's Violin Partita III (BMV 1006).  "Gavotte" means
it is in the form of a French gavotte dance.  It is usually in 4/4, 4/2,
or 2/2 with a nice even rythm.  It usually has an elegant but animated
feel.  "Rondeau" means that a main theme is alternated with contrasting
passages.  In this piece, the main theme is stated in measures 0 through
8 and is restated at measures 16, 40, 64, and 92.

This piece is in 2/2 (alla breve).  ''s mark the quarter note beats and
'.'s mark the eighth note beats.

I haven't had a chance to look it over real well yet, so let me know if
you find any mistakes.


Gavotte En Rondeau (BMV 1006a)          Bach, Johann Sebastian
