*Rhine-e-kin = nickname/sales-cry for Rhinelander beer that we sold on tour.  At Alpine Valley Wisconsin, we (Brian Woodruff, Geoff Valiente, Jeff Breed & myself - I think that was the crew, maybe Scott Lehr was involved - Lehr and LaGreco and I did most of the funnelling) bought 13 cases of long neck bar bottles in thick cardboard cases for $3.8x per 24 btl case, took them to the parking lots/campgrounds and sold them for $1 a piece, or sometimes 2 for $1.50.

I walked around Alpine for 5 days with an Igloo cooler full of about 10 Rhine-e-kins and holding red T-shirts with the black Blue Cheer poster screen.  I sold those for $5 (which was below avg t-shirt price).  We also drank prodigious quantities of Rhine-e-kins, many consumed through a funnel.  

We had one particularly rousing night in the parking lot after a show with guitar playing, drumming, funneling & funnel blowing, then woke up the next morning to righteous west cost hippies pushing the empty Rhinelander bottles, leaking tepid backwash, through Beaullah's pull out windows on the side of the van.  "What the faaaa???" I busted out the door of the van and got into a heated shouting match with some bearded, Guatemalan clad, dreadlocked, patchouli stinkin, whiner and nearly stuck a Rhine-e-kin up his arse.   

"Rhine-e-Kinss, 2 for $1.50!!"  we stole much business from the other beer sellers.  The Rhine-e-kin cry would draw unsuspecting rubes in, believing they may walk away with a cheap, cold Heineken, but we would sell 'em on the quality of the Rhinelander, which was actually quite good for a cheap beer.

http://www.rhinelanderdailynews.com/articles/2009/10/01/news/doc4ac4ccadce9ef138234782.txt

I still have one cardboard Rhinelander case in my house full of speaker cables.
