	Well, here it is. This took a long time so I hope it helps someone. I'm
responsible for the content, except "Tommy's Holiday Camp" by Pete Palmer
[ppalmer@mahogany.cray.com] and "Pinball Wizard" by someone named Marc Hirsh.
So all mistakes in lyrics/chords/notes/keys/typos/etc can be attributed to
myself.
	Please send any proofs to sef@icbr.ufl.edu. I don't know how to archive
(since no one wanted to tell me how) so I'll let someone else do that. 
	I've written things for one guitar (most of the time) but like Jimmy
Page, Townshend likes to layer things, horns, etc. It all sounds pretty close.
But weaknesses abound, so correct as you like. I figured out everything here
and something could be (probably is) wrong.

	Here is as much as I've got for "Pinball Wizard" by the Who. This is 
played on an acoustic (DUH). `e' stands for eighth notes, `s' for sixteenth, 
`q' for quarter, `h' for half, and, yes, even `w' for whole notes. The rapid 
strumming parts can be fairly improvised. The chords in the first 6 bars are 
strummed at a speed so that you can almost hear each individual note. A slide 
is indicated by a `/' for up and `\' for down. A period after a note value 
means dotted. The chord parts are best played with your index finger on the B 
string, middle on the G string (using pinky for pull-offs), and ring fringer 
on the D string, with the thumb wrapping around and playing the low E string. 
Sounds hard, but gets easier with practice.
